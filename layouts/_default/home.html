Why low-carbon websites?

  • Problems

    Internet servers, networks and devices consume a significant amount of power - estimated at 3.7% of all the world's electricity. Most of this electricity comes from non-renewable resources, resulting in significant CO2 emissions.

    Learn more

  • Benefits

    As well as reducing the environmental impact, a low-carbon website will load more quickly so providing a better experience for your site visitors. Search engines prefer faster websites and so, all things being equal, faster websites achieve higher positions in the search results.

UN Sustainability Development Goals

We support the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals and actively work towards making an impact on four key goals:

10 Reduced Inequalities

  • Many parts of the world have much slower internet connections than we do in the UK. Our sustainable, highly efficient websites transfer less data over the internet which means greater access to these websites globally.
  • A significant percentage of people with access to the internet have slower, less powerful devices. Our websites make less demand on devices making them more accessible to a larger number of people across the globe.

12 Responsible Consumption and Production

  • Low-carbon websites are much more efficient than the average site, which means they consume less electricity (and therefore less fossil fuels).
  • We aim to use “green” hosting companies that power their servers using renewable energy sources only.

13 Climate Action

  • We build websites using tools and techniques that keep their carbon footprint as low as possible.
  • Our website is cleaner than 94% of websites tested by Wholegrain Digital.
  • Our website is Climate Positive, accredited by the Eco-friendly Web Alliance.

17 Partnerships for the Goals

  • We work with businesses that want to reduce their carbon footprint.
  • We work in partnership with designers, marekters and content creators that are also aligned with the UN SDGs.
